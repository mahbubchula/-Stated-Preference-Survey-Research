## ğŸ¯ What You Will Learn

<div class="module-card">

### 1. Theoretical Foundations
- Understand when stated preference (SP) methods add value over revealed preference (RP) approaches
- Master random utility theory and discrete choice modeling fundamentals
- Identify appropriate SP applications for transportation policy questions

</div>

<div class="module-card">

### 2. Survey Design Excellence
- Design coherent survey instruments with defensible attributes and levels
- Develop experimental designs using orthogonal and D-efficient methods
- Create choice tasks that minimize respondent burden while maximizing information

</div>

<div class="module-card">

### 3. Professional Implementation
- Deploy SP surveys ethically with robust pilots and sampling plans
- Manage fieldwork logistics, quality assurance, and metadata documentation
- Navigate IRB/ethics approvals and informed consent procedures

</div>

<div class="module-card">

### 4. Advanced Modeling
- Estimate discrete choice models (MNL, mixed logit, latent class)
- Interpret coefficients, elasticities, and willingness-to-pay measures
- Use Biogeme, Apollo, and other professional estimation tools

</div>

<div class="module-card">

### 5. Policy Communication
- Translate findings into actionable policy narratives and recommendations
- Create compelling visualizations and executive dashboards
- Develop implementation roadmaps with scenario analysis

</div>
